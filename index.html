Prices of homes are soaring. Is the end upon us?

Why is the topic of homes so close to our hearts?

80% of the population resides in HDBs in Singapore. While HDB was birthed as a social policy to provide affordable housing to Singapore Citizens/Permanent Residents, opportunistic flat owners have been rapidly selling BTO-es once MOP has been reached for a windfall capital gain. These data visualizations seek to shed light on what the market has been up to recently.

The Minimum Occupation Period(MOP) for HDB flats was introduced to curtail the rapid selling of HDB flats by individuals seeking to make a quick profit. Today, the MOP for resale flats is five years, meaning that flat owners that bought their flats from HDB will have to occupy the flat for five years before it can be sold on the resale market. More info here Using transaction data from Jan 2017 to Dec 2022, we analyze flats with a remaining lease period of 90 years and above. These flats would have recently fulfilled the MOP requirement before the flat can be sold on the resale market and would be the first resale transaction of the flat in the open market (before the next 5-year MOP is reached). This data story is relevant to those seeking to purchase first-generation resale flats, which still bear many similarities to BTO flats except that these potential buyers need not wait for the project to be built. The visualizations may be especially relevant to young couples who are looking for resale flats with a lease to comfortably cover their lifetime.
